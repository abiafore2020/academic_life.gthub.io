Academics

When I was a senior in high school COVID started, so trying to find somewhere I could go was really frustrating. I had a trip to North Carolina planned to tour NC State but they cancelled the touring and I was forced to choose through pictures. In addition to this, all of my tuition money would be going to online classes that I could do from home, so going somewhere out of state and paying tons of money was a bit out of the question.

I ended up doing my freshman year at URI starting off as a Biology Major, which clearly did not work out! I don't like doing heavy memorization, and not because I'm not good at it but it just didn't feel like an interesting way to learn. I love problem solving and was naturally good at math in highschool, so much so I finished 15 credits of math for college while I was in high school. I give all creds to my mom, she's a math teacher and one of the smartest people I know. She ended up taking classes at URI prior to my enrolling there and started teaching computer science at her high school. That and with my dad already working in the field, I tried a coding class there called The Joy of Programming. While I can't always say programming and coding is a joy, I loved that class and the projects we did and it was definitely the reason I switched my major.

From there I transferred to FAU and am hoping to finish my degree in June 2024! I'm considering doing more schooling after I graduate but I'm not sure yet! I want to do an internship this summer (2023) or maybe research during the fall of 2023.
